SECTION 1: DIESEL COMBUSTION EMISSION FACTORS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 Standard CO₂ Emission Factor

    The internationally recognized carbon dioxide emission factor for standard
    diesel fuel combustion is:

        CO₂ Factor = 2.68 kg CO₂ per liter of diesel consumed

    This factor accounts for the complete combustion of diesel fuel (density
    approx. 0.832 kg/L) and is derived from the IPCC Guidelines for National
    Greenhouse Gas Inventories (2006 Revised).

1.2 Adjusted Emission Factors

    Under specific conditions, the effective emission factor may vary:

    - Cold engine start-up (first 10 minutes):     +12% above baseline
    - High-altitude operation (>2000m):             +5% above baseline  
    - Overloaded cargo (>90% capacity):             +8% above baseline
    - Biodiesel blends (B20):                       -15% reduction

1.3 Measurement Units

    All emissions shall be reported in kilograms of CO₂ (kg CO₂).
    Fleet-level aggregation uses metric tonnes (1 tonne = 1000 kg).


SECTION 2: CARBON EMISSION THRESHOLDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 Per-Vehicle Rolling Window Thresholds

    Emissions are monitored using a 10-minute rolling window for each vehicle:

    | Alert Level      | Threshold (kg CO₂ / 10 min) | Action Required          |
    |------------------|-----------------------------|--------------------------|
    | GREEN (Normal)   | 0 – 10.0                    | No action                |
    | YELLOW (Warning) | 10.1 – 15.0                 | Log and monitor          |
    | RED (Violation)  | > 15.0                      | Immediate intervention   |

    The RED threshold of 15 kg CO₂ per 10-minute window is the compliance
    boundary. Exceeding this threshold constitutes a carbon emission violation
    and triggers the EMISSION_ALERT state in the fleet management system.

2.2 Fleet-Wide Daily Thresholds

    Total fleet emissions per day must not exceed:
    
    - Small fleet (1–10 trucks):     500 kg CO₂ / day
    - Medium fleet (11–50 trucks):   2,000 kg CO₂ / day
    - Large fleet (51+ trucks):      8,000 kg CO₂ / day

2.3 Consecutive Violation Policy

    - 1 violation in 1 hour:    Warning issued
    - 3 violations in 1 hour:   Vehicle flagged for route optimization
    - 5 violations in 1 hour:   Mandatory driver re-training notification
    - 10 violations in 24 hrs:  Vehicle suspended from active routes


SECTION 3: IDLE EMISSION REGULATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 Definition of Idle State

    A vehicle is considered "idle" when:
    - Vehicle speed is below 5 km/h, AND
    - Engine is running (fuel_rate > 0), AND
    - Duration exceeds 2 consecutive minutes

3.2 Idle Emission Impact

    Idle trucks consume approximately 1.5–3.0 liters of diesel per hour,
    producing 4.02–8.04 kg CO₂ per hour of idling. This represents a
    significant waste of fuel and unnecessary carbon output.

3.3 Maximum Permitted Idle Time

    - In urban zones:           5 minutes per stop
    - In loading/unloading:     15 minutes per stop
    - In rest areas:            30 minutes (engine off required after)
    - In traffic congestion:    Exempt (auto-detected by system)

3.4 Idle Emission Penalties

    Persistent idling beyond permitted limits incurs:
    - 1st occurrence:   Warning notification to driver
    - 2nd occurrence:   Logged violation (affects eco-score)
    - 3rd occurrence:   Manager escalation


SECTION 4: ROUTE OPTIMIZATION GUIDELINES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 Route Selection Criteria

    Routes must be selected to minimize the following composite metric:

        Route Score = (0.4 × Distance) + (0.3 × Expected_Fuel) + 
                      (0.2 × Traffic_Delay) + (0.1 × Elevation_Change)

    Lower route scores indicate more carbon-efficient paths.

4.2 Dynamic Re-routing

    When a vehicle enters EMISSION_ALERT state, the system should:
    
    1. Evaluate alternative routes within 5 km corridor
    2. Select route with lowest projected carbon output
    3. Notify driver within 60 seconds
    4. Log both original and alternative route for audit

4.3 Speed Optimization for Emissions

    The optimal speed range for diesel truck fuel efficiency is 50–70 km/h.
    
    - Below 40 km/h: Inefficient gear ratios increase fuel consumption
    - 50–70 km/h:    Optimal efficiency zone (lowest CO₂ per km)
    - Above 80 km/h: Aerodynamic drag increases fuel consumption by 20–40%

4.4 Route Deviation Detection

    A route deviation is flagged when:
    - Vehicle position deviates >500m from planned route corridor
    - Deviation persists for >3 minutes
    - No approved detour authorization exists


SECTION 5: ACCELERATION AND DRIVING BEHAVIOR IMPACT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Acceleration Variance Threshold

    Speed variance within a 5-minute window is computed as the statistical
    variance of instantaneous speed samples. High variance indicates
    aggressive driving (frequent acceleration/deceleration).

    | Variance Level | Speed Variance (km/h)² | Classification          |
    |----------------|------------------------|-------------------------|
    | Low            | 0 – 50                 | Smooth driving          |
    | Medium         | 51 – 150               | Moderate variability    |
    | High           | > 150                  | Aggressive driving      |

5.2 Emission Impact of Aggressive Driving

    Studies show that aggressive driving patterns increase fuel consumption
    by 15–30% compared to smooth, steady-speed driving. This translates to:

    - Additional 0.8–2.0 kg CO₂ per 10-minute window
    - Increased mechanical wear (brake pads, tires)
    - Higher accident risk

5.3 Driver Coaching Triggers

    When a vehicle exhibits:
    - Speed variance > 150 for 3 consecutive windows:   Coaching alert
    - Average acceleration > 2.5 m/s²:                  Hard acceleration flag
    - Deceleration events > 3.0 m/s²:                   Hard braking flag


SECTION 6: COMPLIANCE REPORTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 Real-Time Compliance Dashboard

    All fleet operators must maintain a real-time monitoring system displaying:
    - Per-vehicle carbon emission (current window)
    - Fleet aggregate emissions
    - Active alerts and violations
    - Eco-score rankings

6.2 Audit Trail Requirements

    All emission data, alerts, and state transitions must be logged with:
    - Timestamp (UTC)
    - Vehicle identifier
    - GPS coordinates
    - Associated metric values
    - Alert/state information
