DIVISION 1: BHARAT STAGE VI (BS-VI) EMISSION STANDARDS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 Applicability

    All commercial vehicles operating on Indian public highways must comply
    with Bharat Stage VI (BS-VI) emission standards as mandated by the
    Ministry of Road Transport and Highways (MoRTH).

    BS-VI standards apply to:
    - Heavy Commercial Vehicles (HCV) > 12 tonnes GVW
    - Medium Commercial Vehicles (MCV) 3.5 - 12 tonnes GVW
    - Light Commercial Vehicles (LCV) < 3.5 tonnes GVW

1.2 BS-VI Pollutant Limits (Diesel)

    | Pollutant                | Limit (g/kWh)         | Test Cycle    |
    |--------------------------|-----------------------|---------------|
    | Carbon Monoxide (CO)     | 1.5                   | WHTC          |
    | Hydrocarbons (HC)        | 0.13                  | WHTC          |
    | Nitrogen Oxides (NOx)    | 0.4                   | WHTC          |
    | Particulate Matter (PM)  | 0.01                  | WHTC          |
    | Particle Number (PN)     | 8.0 x 10^11 /kWh     | WHTC          |
    | Ammonia (NH3)            | 10 ppm                | WHTC          |

1.3 On-Board Diagnostics (OBD)

    All BS-VI trucks must have OBD-II systems that continuously monitor:
    - Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) status
    - Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) performance
    - Exhaust Gas Recirculation (EGR) functionality
    - Diesel Oxidation Catalyst (DOC) efficiency

    OBD malfunction indicators must activate when emissions exceed 1.5x
    of the type-approval limit for any regulated pollutant.

1.4 Real Driving Emissions (RDE)

    Fleet operators must demonstrate compliance under real driving conditions:
    - Urban driving (< 60 km/h): Minimum 29% of trip time
    - Rural driving (60-90 km/h): Minimum 23% of trip time
    - Motorway driving (> 90 km/h): Minimum 23% of trip time

    RDE conformity factor: 1.5x of WHTC limits allowed during transition.


DIVISION 2: POLLUTION UNDER CONTROL (PUC) CERTIFICATE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 Requirements

    Every commercial vehicle must possess a valid PUC certificate:
    - Renewal: Every 6 months (BS-VI vehicles)
    - Renewal: Every 3 months (Pre-BS-VI vehicles)
    - Testing at authorized PUC centers only

2.2 PUC Test Parameters

    | Parameter         | BS-VI Limit              | Pre-BS-VI Limit      |
    |-------------------|--------------------------|----------------------|
    | CO at idle        | 0.30%                    | 0.50%                |
    | HC at idle        | 100 ppm                  | 1500 ppm             |
    | Smoke opacity     | 25 HSU                   | 65 HSU               |
    | Lambda            | 0.97 - 1.03              | N/A                  |

2.3 Penalties for Non-Compliance

    Under the Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 2019:
    - First offense:   Rs. 10,000 fine
    - Repeat offense:  Rs. 10,000 fine + vehicle impoundment
    - Operating without PUC: Rs. 10,000 fine + driving license suspension


DIVISION 3: CARBON FOOTPRINT REPORTING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 Mandatory Carbon Reporting

    Effective April 2025, all fleet operators with > 20 vehicles must:
    1. Calculate and report monthly fleet carbon emissions
    2. Submit quarterly reports to the State Pollution Control Board
    3. Maintain digital emission logs with GPS-tagged data

3.2 Carbon Calculation Methodology

    Approved formula for diesel vehicles:

        CO2 (kg) = Fuel Consumed (liters) x 2.68

    For mixed fuel fleets:
    | Fuel Type        | CO2 Factor (kg/liter)    |
    |------------------|--------------------------|
    | Diesel           | 2.68                     |
    | Petrol           | 2.31                     |
    | CNG              | 2.75 (per kg)            |
    | LNG              | 2.75 (per kg)            |
    | Biodiesel B20    | 2.28                     |
    | Electric         | 0.82 (per kWh, grid)     |

3.3 Annual Carbon Budget

    Fleet operators must plan annual carbon budgets:

    | Fleet Size    | Annual Budget (tonnes CO2)  | Per-Vehicle Limit     |
    |---------------|-----------------------------|-----------------------|
    | 1-20 vehicles | No mandatory limit          | Voluntary reporting   |
    | 21-50         | 1,500 tonnes/year           | 30 tonnes/vehicle/yr  |
    | 51-100        | 2,500 tonnes/year           | 25 tonnes/vehicle/yr  |
    | 100+          | 4,000 tonnes/year           | 20 tonnes/vehicle/yr  |


DIVISION 4: GREEN CORRIDOR REGULATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 Designated Green Corridors

    The following National Highway corridors have been designated as 
    Green Corridors with enhanced emission monitoring:

    | Corridor                    | NH    | Active Since |
    |-----------------------------|-------|-------------|
    | Delhi - Jaipur              | NH48  | Jan 2025    |
    | Mumbai - Pune Expressway    | MPEW  | Mar 2025    |
    | Bangalore - Chennai         | NH48  | Jun 2025    |
    | Delhi - Agra (Yamuna Exp)   | YEAY  | Sep 2025    |
    | Kolkata - Patna             | NH19  | Jan 2026    |

4.2 Green Corridor Requirements

    Vehicles operating on Green Corridors must:
    1. Maintain speed within 50-80 km/h range
    2. Not exceed idle time of 5 minutes at any non-designated stop
    3. Comply with real-time emission monitoring via GPS-enabled OBD
    4. Display valid Green Transport Compliance sticker
    5. Report any emission threshold breach within 15 minutes

4.3 Penalties on Green Corridors

    Enhanced penalties apply:
    - Emission threshold breach:    Rs. 25,000 per instance
    - Unauthorized idling > 10 min: Rs. 5,000 per instance
    - Speeding > 80 km/h:          Rs. 10,000 + suspension
    - Missing compliance sticker:   Rs. 15,000


DIVISION 5: ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACT ASSESSMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Fleet Environmental Score

    Each fleet receives a quarterly Environmental Score (E-Score):

        E-Score = W1*EmissionScore + W2*FuelScore + W3*ComplianceScore

    Where:
    - W1 = 0.4 (Emission Weight)
    - W2 = 0.3 (Fuel Efficiency Weight)
    - W3 = 0.3 (Compliance Weight)

5.2 E-Score Ratings

    | Score Range | Rating       | Consequence                          |
    |-------------|-------------|--------------------------------------|
    | 90 - 100    | Platinum    | Tax incentives, priority permits      |
    | 75 - 89     | Gold        | Standard operations                  |
    | 60 - 74     | Silver      | Improvement plan required (90 days)  |
    | 40 - 59     | Bronze      | Mandatory audit + driver retraining  |
    | < 40        | Red         | Operating license review             |

5.3 Improvement Mandates

    Fleets with E-Score below 60 must:
    1. Submit improvement plan within 30 days
    2. Implement corrective measures within 90 days
    3. Achieve minimum Silver rating within 180 days
    4. Failure results in operating license suspension
