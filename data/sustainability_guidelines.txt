CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION TO ECO-DRIVING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 Purpose

    This document establishes best practices for eco-driving within commercial
    fleet operations. Implementing these guidelines can reduce fleet fuel
    consumption by 10–25% and proportionally decrease carbon emissions.

1.2 Scope

    These guidelines apply to all commercial diesel truck operators within
    the GreenRoute AI monitored fleet. Compliance is tracked through the
    real-time eco-score system.


CHAPTER 2: OPTIMAL SPEED MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

2.1 Target Speed Range

    The optimal speed range for diesel trucks to maximize fuel efficiency is:

        >>> OPTIMAL SPEED: 50 – 70 km/h <<<

    Within this range:
    - Engine operates at peak thermal efficiency
    - Gear ratios are optimized for load
    - Aerodynamic drag is manageable
    - Tire rolling resistance is balanced

2.2 Speed Impact on Fuel Consumption

    | Speed Range   | Fuel Efficiency Impact             | CO₂ Impact       |
    |---------------|-------------------------------------|------------------|
    | < 30 km/h     | +35% fuel usage (low gear)         | High per km      |
    | 30–50 km/h    | +10% fuel usage (suboptimal)       | Moderate per km  |
    | 50–70 km/h    | Baseline (optimal)                 | Lowest per km    |
    | 70–90 km/h    | +15% fuel usage (drag increase)    | Elevated per km  |
    | > 90 km/h     | +35% fuel usage (high drag)        | Very high per km |

2.3 Speed Consistency

    Maintaining a consistent speed is more important than maintaining a fast
    speed. Drivers should:
    
    - Use cruise control on highways when available
    - Anticipate traffic flow to avoid sudden speed changes  
    - Allow longer following distances to reduce reactive braking
    - Gradually adjust speed when approaching hills


CHAPTER 3: ACCELERATION AND BRAKING PRACTICES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 Smooth Acceleration

    Best practices for acceleration:
    
    - Accelerate gradually (target: < 1.5 m/s²)
    - Upshift early to keep RPM in the efficiency band (1200–1600 RPM)
    - Avoid "jackrabbit" starts from stops
    - Use momentum to carry the vehicle through speed transitions

3.2 Reducing Sudden Braking

    Sudden braking wastes the kinetic energy that fuel was consumed to create.
    
    Prevention strategies:
    - Maintain safe following distance (minimum 4 seconds for trucks)
    - Scan ahead for red lights, stop signs, and traffic slowdowns
    - Use engine braking (downshifting) before applying wheel brakes
    - Coast to decelerate when approaching known stops

3.3 Impact Quantification

    Each hard braking event (deceleration > 3.0 m/s²) followed by
    re-acceleration wastes approximately:
    
    - 0.05–0.15 liters of diesel
    - 0.13–0.40 kg of CO₂
    
    A driver averaging 20 hard brakes per trip wastes 1–3 liters of fuel
    per trip unnecessarily.


CHAPTER 4: IDLE TIME MINIMIZATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

4.1 The Cost of Idling

    A typical diesel truck at idle consumes:
    
    - Fuel:     1.5 – 3.0 liters per hour
    - CO₂:     4.02 – 8.04 kg per hour
    - Cost:     ₹120 – ₹240 per hour (at ₹80/liter diesel)

    Over a fleet of 10 trucks, even 30 minutes of unnecessary daily idling
    per truck results in:
    
    - 75 – 150 liters of wasted fuel per month
    - 201 – 402 kg of unnecessary CO₂ per month

4.2 Idle Reduction Strategies

    1. Turn off engine during stops exceeding 2 minutes
    2. Use auxiliary power units (APU) for cabin climate control
    3. Pre-plan stops to minimize wait times at loading docks
    4. Coordinate with dispatchers to avoid early arrivals
    5. Use shore power at equipped facilities

4.3 Monitoring

    The GreenRoute AI system automatically detects idle states when:
    - Speed < 5 km/h for extended periods
    - Engine running (fuel_rate > 0)
    
    Idle events are logged and affect the driver's eco-score.


CHAPTER 5: CARGO WEIGHT AND DISTRIBUTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

5.1 Weight Impact on Fuel Consumption

    Every additional 1,000 kg of cargo increases fuel consumption by
    approximately 5–8%. Optimal loading practices include:

    - Load only required cargo (avoid unnecessary weight)
    - Distribute weight evenly across axles
    - Center heavy items low and toward the vehicle center
    - Secure cargo to prevent shifting (shifting load affects aerodynamics)

5.2 Optimal Load Ranges

    | Cargo Weight  | Load Factor | Fuel Impact      | Recommendation        |
    |---------------|-------------|-------------------|-----------------------|
    | < 5,000 kg    | Light       | Baseline          | Consider consolidation|
    | 5,000–12,000  | Moderate    | +10–15% over empty| Optimal range         |
    | 12,000–18,000 | Heavy       | +20–30% over empty| Monitor fuel closely  |
    | > 18,000 kg   | Overweight  | +35%+ over empty  | Review necessity      |

5.3 Aerodynamic Cargo Practices

    - Use aerodynamic fairings on flatbed trailers
    - Cover open cargo to reduce drag
    - Stack cargo to maintain a smooth profile
    - Remove empty roof racks and accessories when not in use


CHAPTER 6: VEHICLE MAINTENANCE FOR EFFICIENCY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

6.1 Tire Maintenance

    Under-inflated tires increase fuel consumption by 1% per 10 PSI below
    optimal pressure.
    
    - Check tire pressure weekly (cold tires)
    - Maintain manufacturer-recommended pressure
    - Replace tires when tread depth < 3mm
    - Use low rolling resistance tires where possible

6.2 Engine Maintenance

    - Change engine oil at recommended intervals
    - Replace air filters every 20,000 km or as needed
    - Keep fuel injectors clean
    - Maintain cooling system efficiency
    - Address engine warning lights immediately

6.3 Aerodynamic Maintenance

    - Repair body damage that disrupts airflow
    - Ensure mud flaps and fairings are intact
    - Keep roof deflectors properly adjusted for trailer height


CHAPTER 7: ECO-SCORE SYSTEM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

7.1 Eco-Score Calculation

    The driver/vehicle eco-score is computed as:

        eco_score = (avg_speed / avg_fuel_rate) - (carbon_emission / 10)

    Higher scores indicate better eco-performance.

7.2 Score Interpretation

    | Eco-Score Range | Rating    | Interpretation                      |
    |-----------------|-----------|-------------------------------------|
    | > 8.0           | Excellent | Top performer, minimal emissions    |
    | 5.0 – 8.0       | Good      | Meets standards, room to improve    |
    | 2.0 – 5.0       | Fair      | Below expectations, coaching needed |
    | < 2.0           | Poor      | Immediate intervention required     |

7.3 Score Improvement Tips

    1. Maintain speed in the 50–70 km/h range
    2. Minimize idle time
    3. Drive smoothly (reduce speed variance)
    4. Optimize cargo loading
    5. Keep vehicle well-maintained
